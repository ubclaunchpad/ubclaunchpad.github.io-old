About Us

Our motivation

A lot of students at UBC know code. Most don’t know application development, that’s something you are supposed to do by yourself. Until now, there was no club on campus to code together or any club that really focused on holistic software development. We wanted to create a community centered around learning, applying and building your portfolio by working on projects in teams rather than alone. This enables students to not only be social but the ability to build much larger scale projects. Launch Pad facilitates this process, by providing the mentorship, education, tools, business, and connections.

The paradigms we are attempting to change

We are in engineering – should be easy for us to get a job right? Not really – on top of our massive course load we need to spend a lot of time outside of school building a portfolio. Without this, good luck finding a job that’s worthwile. Prior to us, the only way to do this was to sit down on weekends and perhaps pay for external courses to learn web, iOS, or Android development just so employers will view you as competitive. Why should we have to pay for skills or have to go through courses that have no relevance to industry.

With Launch Pad, we teach you courses that would otherwise cost thousands for free. We the immediately put you onto meaningful projects suited to your caliber and give you real mentors to help you every step of the way.

“You need experience to get experience”

Lots of great ideas spawn during university. Take a look at the technology you use today, almost a majority of them come from ideas or people that happened to connect during university. We think that this is just the tip of the iceberg, there are far more ideas that need to be developed and see the light of day. By doing this, we create a community that focused on solving problems, not getting stuck because of funding or a lack of network.

We are a social venture focused on impact, so we don’t let these trivial matters stand in the way. We create applications that are far cheaper than any market rate you will find. We also provide you some of the best and brightest at UBC to see your idea through. Then, we connect you with the right people to launch your idea into a business and hopefully an impactful solution in the future.

“If you have a great idea to solve a problem – you need to know a great coder or have lots of money”

This was the case… 15 years ago. With modern technologies, you can do what would have taken a developer months to do, in minutes. Even with this, the mentality amonst most people is that technology is simply to complicated.

We break the barriers by providing education to the public about code, and how you don’t need to be a genius in order to use it. Tech is becoming more and more about abstraction away from the details making it seem like a complicated mess under the hood when you stare at the hundreds of lines of code. However, this is only an illusion, within a few hours, we can show you everything you need to know about creating a webpage not using those clunky drag-and-drop interfaced. Through our workshops, you will be amazed how easy things really are.

“Technology is complicated. I need lots of years of practice, spend lots of money, and be technical in nature in order to code.”
